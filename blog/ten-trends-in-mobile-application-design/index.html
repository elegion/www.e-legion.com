Ten Trends in Mobile Application Design 2020

The e-Legion team gathered expert opinions of product managers and UX designers from Tinkoff Bank, Tele2, Cherdak, UX Planet, FL.ru and other companies to find out how user interaction will change and what awaits mobile interfaces in 2020.

1. Zero UI. Simplification, minimalism, brevity

The simpler it is, the clearer it is for the user. All experts called the simplification of interfaces or Zero UI the main trend of next year. Nothing should distract the user from the product Functionality comes to the fore. More space appears between the elements, colors become brighter and purer, simple geometry prevails over complex graphics and illustrations.

Brevity will be compensated with work on typography and the use of asymmetric grids. However, minimalism will be not only visual, but will also contain information We are already trying not to overload the users, minimize the content, leaving only the most important parts.

Zero UI will be trending ‚Äî an ‚Äúinvisible‚Äù design, clear from the context. The most famous example of this interface is Instagram. In applications with Zero UI, the user can focus on the content and feel the value of the application itself.

According to Udonis, 2 out of 5 users regularly use voice devices. This is a promising trend, the development of which will lead to even more simplified interfaces.

Voice assistants are a new type of mobile interaction, and they are just like mobile phones in the 2000s ‚Äî no one believed that it could significantly push computers out of the market. The future is for a person who gets everything from the mobile device just by the voice commands. The future is for solving all of your problems without even looking at the interface. An audio device is a new type of human-network interaction. It will take in-depth analytics to identify the principles of behavior of an ordinary user. We won‚Äôt even notice how we moved from now to this future, where everything is solved with a voice command.

Applications will know what you want better than you. According to eMarketer, 35% of users want apps and websites to be more personalized. This allows you to display relevant products, services and content. Personalization has already been implemented in social networks, media, banking services and online stores. Spotify offer playlists based on preferences, ASOS shows clothes based on your style, Instagram, Facebook form a ‚Äúsmart‚Äù news feed. Accumulated data and predictive analytics allow you to provide more relevant content. Naturally, this trend will continue to influence the experience of interacting with services and devices. Customization extends UX and allows you to control interaction: adjust the theme and order of elements, hide and add blocks so that you only see the most useful and interesting parts. For example, Tinkoff app lets you hide cards and accounts, Telegram lets you change the design completely, Facebook lets you customize the news feed, and My Tele2 lets you change the colors and names of cards with numbers.

Business rushed to introduce augmented reality wherever possible, but few found its practical usage. Now the technology is no longer a hype and has matured. It was introduced to retail and geolocation applications. For example, Google Maps, ASOS and Nike.

In 2020, AR will be ready for practical applications.
